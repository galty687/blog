One Method to Predict the Usability of Technical Documentation

Background

Usability testing plays a vital role in the process of technical writing, which could be used to discover the potential problems neglected in the design phase. The usability score is often used to reflect the quality of the documentation and predict the satisfaction the end users may feel when they use them.

Many technical writers hope they could have a testing team to test the usability whenever they finish a document or a piece of information. However, conducting usability testing is expensive and time-consuming, which prevents many technical writers from using usability testing as a method to improve their information design.

Goal

In this dissertation, I am proposing a model to predict the usability based on usability indicators or metrics. With this model, technical writers could find whether their documentation is improving. The administrative team could also use this model to evaluate the documents.

Research Stages

The research project is divided into four main stages, they are:

  1. A literature review of the problem.
  2. How people use technical documentation?
  3. What makes technical documentation usable?
  4. Work out a predictive model and validate it.

Literature review of the problem

In this phase, I will try to find whether there are similar researches. What kind of results has been found in this research area. What I have found till now is that not much literature deal with the same problem as I want to solve. Most of the literature talk about:

  1. Guidelines to write high usability documents
  2. How to conduct the usability testing
  3. Usability Metrics:
  4. Effectiveness
  5. Efficiency
  6. Satisfaction
  7. The list will be continued.

How people use technical documentation

Documents are designed to help people complete tasks. It's necessary to investigate how people need and use information. The results could be used to decide which factors are key factors affecting the usability perceived by the users. A Partial list of information behavior detected:

  1. People prefer to try things out before they consult the documents
  2. When they encounter problems, they prefer to ask people nearby, call the people they trust or just Google research. After all these means fail, they try to get the help from the documents.
  3. When they use technical documentation, they scan to find the information they need.
  4. The list will be continued.

What makes technical documentation usable

When we know how people use technical documentation, we could detect the key factors which may affect the usability. The factors detected till now:

Effectiveness:

  1. Task-Oriented. The percentage of task oriented sentence in the whole document.
  2. Easy to understand. Readability Score.
  3. Accuracy. The percentage of errors detected by MS Word.

Efficiency

  1. Easy to Navigate. The coverage of Index terms. Index Score.
  2. Easy to Scan. heading sentence number / total sentences

Satisfaction

  1. Human Interest Index. HII Score.
  2. Layout attraction. A score from website usability research.

The factors will be optimized with the development of this research.

Work out a predictive model and validate it

In this project, I will do the summative evaluation of documentation usability rather than the formative evaluation. My goal is to predict the final usability score. If the automatically calculated score is strongly relative the score concluded from human usability testing, the model is already usefully for summative evaluation.

In this phase, I will conduct a series of documentation usability testing and get usability scores for every document. Then I will use the factors founded above to work out a predictive model.
